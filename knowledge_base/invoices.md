# Invoices

**Description**: Essential commercial documents issued by a seller to a buyer, detailing the products, quantities, and agreed prices for goods or services.

1. **Invoice Number**: A unique sequential identifier assigned to each invoice for tracking, reference, and organizational purposes.
2. **Billing Date**: The specific date when the invoice is generated and issued to the client or customer.
3. **Due Date**: The deadline by which the buyer must complete the payment to avoid penalties or late fees.
4. **Vendor Information**: The complete contact details of the seller, including business name, address, phone number, email, and tax identification numbers.
5. **Customer Information**: The contact details of the buyer or client to whom the goods or services were provided.
6. **Item Description**: A clear and specific breakdown of each product or service rendered, preventing confusion over what is being billed.
7. **Unit Price**: The cost of a single unit of the product or service provided, establishing the basis for total cost calculation.
8. **Quantity**: The exact number of units or hours provided for each item listed on the invoice.
9. **Subtotal**: The combined cost of all items or services before any taxes, discounts, or shipping fees are applied.
10. **Tax Calculation**: The applicable sales tax, VAT, or GST added to the subtotal, dictated by local regulations and the nature of the transaction.
11. **Total Amount**: The final, comprehensive sum that the buyer is obligated to pay, including the subtotal, taxes, and any other fees.
12. **Payment Terms**: The conditions under which payment is expected, such as "Net 30" (payment due in 30 days) or "Due upon receipt."
13. **Late Payment Policy**: Defined penalties or interest charges that will be applied if the customer fails to remit payment by the specified due date.
14. **Payment Methods**: Accepted ways for the customer to pay, which may include bank transfers, credit cards, checks, or digital payment gateways.
15. **Invoice Reference Codes**: Additional codes, such as Purchase Order (PO) numbers, required by the buyer's accounts payable department for processing.
16. **Discount Handling**: Any price reductions applied to the invoice, detailed clearly to show the original price and the discounted amount.
17. **Credit Notes**: Documents issued to correct an overcharge or account for returned goods, effectively reducing the amount owed by the buyer.
18. **Billing Cycles**: The regular intervals at which recurring services are invoiced, such as monthly, quarterly, or annually.
19. **Electronic Invoices**: Digital formats of invoices (e-invoices) that streamline delivery, processing, and integrate directly into accounting software.
20. **Invoice Archiving**: The systematic storage of paid and unpaid invoices for future reference, accounting audits, and corporate tax compliance needs.
