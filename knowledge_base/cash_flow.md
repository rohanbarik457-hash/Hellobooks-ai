# Cash Flow

**Description**: A statement tracking the exact movement of cash into and out of the business over a period to correctly assess total financial liquidity.

1. **Cash Inflows**: All actual money coming into the business, primarily generated through customer sales, acquiring loans, or receiving external investment capital.
2. **Cash Outflows**: All physical money leaving the business, representing payments for daily expenses, asset purchases, debt settlements, and dividend distributions.
3. **Operating Cash Flow**: Cash generated directly from the company's core operational activities, reflecting if the firm can organically sustain its standard operations.
4. **Investing Cash Flow**: Cash activity directly related to the purchase or sale of long-term assets such as real estate, equipment, or external investments.
5. **Financing Cash Flow**: Cash exchanges directly between the company and its primary owners or corporate creditors, including issuing stock or repaying principal loans.
6. **Cash Receipts**: The tangible receipt of physical money or immediate transfers from customers, distinct from revenue recorded on account.
7. **Supplier Payments**: The act of formally transferring cash to vendors and authorized suppliers to settle accounts payable for delivered materials and inventory.
8. **Salary Payments**: The highly regular cash disbursements made directly to reliably compensate company employees for their labor, time, and contracted services.
9. **Loan Repayments**: The cash outflow directly and exclusively associated with reducing the principal balance of outstanding institutional or private debt.
10. **Equipment Purchases**: Significant cash outflows officially classified under investing activities whenever the developing business selectively acquires new machinery or hardware tools.
11. **Owner Investments**: Immediate cash inflows specifically resulting from founders, owners, or minority shareholders actively injecting personal funds into the business entity.
12. **Cash Forecasting**: Methodically anticipating all future cash inflows and structural outflows to adequately prepare for possible operational shortfalls and preserve liquidity.
13. **Liquidity Monitoring**: Constantly assessing the company's genuine ability to quickly and reliably access cash in order to meet immediate short-term corporate obligations.
14. **Working Capital Management**: The strategic administrative handling of current assets versus current liabilities to ensure the business fundamentally operates smoothly.
15. **Cash Reserves**: Holding a predetermined surplus of highly liquid cash to serve as a designated buffer against unexpected external expenses or broad economic downturns.
16. **Financial Stability**: A secure state wherein the business dependably generates positive cash flow, consistently covering all operating expenses effortlessly.
17. **Cash Cycle**: The precise tracking of turnaround time it takes for a company to convert baseline resource investments completely back into actual cash.
18. **Cash Shortage Prevention**: Forcefully implementing strict credit protocols and austere operational expense management to proactively avoid scenarios lacking liquid bill funds.
19. **Daily Cash Tracking**: Rigorously monitoring all business bank balances and daily receipt trends versus general disbursements to meticulously maintain tight capital control.
20. **Cash Flow Reporting**: The formal recurring preparation of the cash flow statement, immediately providing crucial structural insights beyond standard theoretical ledger metrics.
