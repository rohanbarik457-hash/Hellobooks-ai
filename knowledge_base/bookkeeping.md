# Bookkeeping

**Description**: Fundamental principles and practices of recording daily financial transactions for accurate business tracking.

1. **Recording Transactions**: Systematically logging every financial exchange, including purchases, sales, receipts, and payments.
2. **Double-Entry Accounting**: A system where every transaction affects at least two accounts, ensuring the accounting equation (Assets = Liabilities + Equity) remains in balance.
3. **General Ledger**: The master record of all financial accounts, providing a comprehensive history of a company's financial transactions.
4. **Journal Entries**: The initial logs of individual transactions, detailing the date, amounts to be debited and credited, and a brief description.
5. **Accounts Payable**: The record of money a company owes to its suppliers or vendors for goods and services received on credit.
6. **Accounts Receivable**: The tracking of money owed to a company by its customers for goods or services delivered but not yet paid for.
7. **Reconciliation**: The process of comparing internal financial records with external statements (like bank statements) to ensure consistency and identify discrepancies.
8. **Trial Balance**: A report listing the balances of all general ledger accounts at a specific point in time to verify that total debits equal total credits.
9. **Financial Record Accuracy**: Maintaining precise and error-free logs to ensure reliable reporting and informed business decision-making.
10. **Expense Tracking**: Monitoring outgoing funds to manage cash flow, create budgets, and identify areas for cost reduction.
11. **Revenue Recording**: Documenting incoming funds from primary business activities to accurately evaluate profitability and growth.
12. **Chart of Accounts**: An organized list of all financial accounts used by a business, categorized into assets, liabilities, equity, revenue, and expenses.
13. **Audit Preparation**: Keeping chronological and organized records with supporting documents to facilitate financial examinations by internal or external auditors.
14. **Daily Transaction Logging**: The routine practice of entering financial data promptly to prevent backlog, errors, and loss of financial visibility.
15. **Tax Documentation**: Compiling and retaining necessary financial records, receipts, and invoices required for accurate tax filing and compliance.
16. **Asset Tracking**: Recording the acquisition, depreciation, and disposal of company-owned resources holding economic value.
17. **Liability Tracking**: Monitoring ongoing financial obligations, loan balances, and accrued expenses to ensure timely settlements.
18. **Financial Transparency**: Clear and accurate accounting practices that provide stakeholders with a truthful view of the company's financial health.
19. **Internal Control Practices**: Implementing checks and balances, such as segregating duties, to prevent fraud, theft, and financial mismanagement.
20. **Year-End Closing**: The process of finalizing financial records for a fiscal year, zeroing out temporary accounts, and carrying forward permanent balances.
